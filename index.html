About Me.

I am a BASI Level 4 (ISTD) qualified snowboard instructor. BASI is the British association of snow sports instructors (www.basi.org.uk) and level 4 is their top level.


I have so far taught 12 winters working full time as a snowboard instructor teaching adults and kids, from beginners up to training towards the higher level of being an instructor and competing as a professional. I have spent:

  • 6 winters in the Austrian Alps
  • 3 winters in New Zealand
  • 1 winter in Switzerland
  • 1 winter in Scotland
  • 1 winter in Japan

During this time I spent 3 years as a freestyle coach at the illustrious Absolut Park’s official freestyle school – Absolut school. As a freestyle coach at Cardrona’s high performance centre (HPC).
And I have trained instructors, especially in New Zealand where I played a part in a 10 week instructor training centre (ITC) programme which helps people learn how to become an instructor. I also hold a European mountain safety (EMS) qualification gained through BASI which allows me to take people off-piste ski touring and split boarding in non-glaciated terrains.


Furthermore, I also have a BSc honours degree in Sports Psychology from the Open University, and I am able to speak and teach snowboarding in 5 languages – English, Dutch, German, Spanish and French.
